Vietnamese High-Impact Chicken Thighs

Bone-In, Skin-On · Blender Marinade · Pan-Sear + Convection Finish
Serves ~6–8
Active ~20 min
Sear 8–11 min
Oven 20–25 min
Marinate 4–24 h
Air-Dry 4–12 h

Ingredients

Chicken
4 lbsbone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
Blender Marinade — Liquids
6 tbspfish sauce
2 tbsplow-sodium tamari
2 tbsprice vinegar
2 limesjuiced (~60 ml)
1 tbsphoney
Blender Marinade — Aromatics
6 clovesgarlic
2 mediumshallots
2 tbspchopped lemongrass (white part only)
2 tspfresh ginger
Blender Marinade — Maximizers
1 tspMSG
1 tspfive-spice powder
2 tspfreshly cracked black pepper
1 tspchili flakes
Cooking
1–2 tspneutral oil (for searing)
Serving (optional)
Fresh lime, mint, cilantro, or Thai basil

Blender Marinade

Add all marinade ingredients to blender. Blend 30–45 seconds until smooth. Do not over-aerate.

Marinate

Loosen skin from meat with fingers.

Rub marinade under skin and on meat side.

Lightly wipe excess from outer skin.

Refrigerate 4–24 hours.

Air-Dry (Critical for Crisp Skin)

Remove thighs from marinade.

Pat skin completely dry.

Place uncovered on rack in fridge 4–12 hours.

Sear

  • Heavy skillet (cast iron ideal), medium heat
  • 1–2 tsp neutral oil
  • Place thighs skin-side down
  • Do not move for 6–8 minutes
  • Flip and cook 2–3 minutes
  • Pour off excess rendered fat (leave thin film)

Oven Finish

Your convection oven runs ~25°F lower than the set temperature.
  • Set: 400°F convection (actual ~375°F)
  • Cook 20–25 minutes
  • Pull at 180–185°F internal temperature

Adjustments

Skin browns too quickly Lower to 350°F convection (set 375)
Skin needs more crisp at end Broil 1–2 min, watch carefully

Serving

  • Fresh lime squeeze
  • Mint, cilantro, or Thai basil
  • One thigh with reduced drippings
  • No glaze needed
Do not pull at 165°F. Dark meat improves significantly between 175–185°F as collagen softens and fat fully renders.

Nutrition (per thigh, 1 of ~6 servings)

310 kcal
28 g Protein
3 g Carbs
20 g Fat
Nutrition estimate based on USDA data for bone-in skin-on chicken thigh (cooked, skin eaten), with marinade ingredients distributed across ~6 thighs. Fat accounts for skin rendered during sear — actual fat depends on how much you pour off. The marinade contributes most of the sodium (~850 mg/serving, primarily from fish sauce and tamari) and minimal carbs (~3 g from honey, shallots, and lime juice). Bone weight is excluded. For tighter MacroFactor logging, weigh individual thighs after cooking and log by weight — a cooked bone-in thigh typically yields ~60–65% edible meat+skin relative to raw weight.
