Instant Pot Chicken Stock

Saved Bones · Defatted · Reduced · Unseasoned
Yield ~2 qt (8 cups)
Serving 1 cup (240 ml)
Pressure 70 min
Reduction ~30%
Total ~24 h (incl. chill)

Ingredients

~2–3 lbspreviously cooked chicken bones (carcasses, drumstick/thigh bones, etc.)
~10 cupswater (to 6 qt Instant Pot max fill line)
These bones have already given up most of their meat protein during the first cook. What remains is residual collagen in the connective tissue and joints. The stock will be lighter in body and protein than the fresh drumstick version.

Method

Cook

Place saved bones in 6 qt Instant Pot. Fill with water to max fill line.

Seal lid. High pressure, 70 minutes. Allow natural pressure release.

Strain stock through mesh strainer. Discard spent bones.

Defat

Refrigerate overnight. Remove and discard fat cap (will be thinner than fresh drumstick version).

Reduce

Return defatted stock to pot. Simmer uncovered on stovetop until reduced by approximately 30%.

Final yield: ~2 quarts (8 cups). Transfer to 2 quart-size Ball jars.

Gel check: This version may gel softly or not at all — that's expected. Previously cooked bones have already surrendered much of their collagen during the first cook. A soft gel means ~5 g protein/cup. No gel at all suggests closer to 3 g/cup, and you should adjust down.

Nutrition (per 1 cup / 240 ml serving)

25 kcal
5 g Protein
0 g Carbs
0.5 g Fat
How these numbers were derived: Previously cooked bones have already lost a substantial portion of their extractable collagen during the first cook. Research on second-extraction bone broths and reused carcass stocks consistently show roughly 40–60% less gelatin yield compared to first-extraction from fresh bones. Starting from the fresh drumstick version's ~9 g/cup estimate and applying ~45% reduction for second extraction lands at ~5 g/cup protein. This is consistent with the USDA FoodData Central entry for home-prepared chicken stock (FDC #172884) which reports ~2.5 g protein/cup — but that entry includes vegetable dilution and assumes no reduction step. The 30% reduction concentrates your stock beyond that baseline. Fat is minimal after cold defatting. No carbs without vegetables. These are best-effort estimates — gel firmness is your most reliable real-world proxy for protein content.
