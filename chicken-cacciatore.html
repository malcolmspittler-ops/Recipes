Chicken Cacciatore

Pollo alla Cacciatora · Hunter's Style · Ligurian
Serves4
Braise~75 min total
MethodStovetop Braise
Gluten-Free✓ Naturally
This is not the Americanized version. No bell peppers, no mushrooms. Ligurian cacciatore uses olives, capers, white wine, and rosemary. The flavors are briny, herby, and complex rather than sweet. The sauce must be reduced properly at the end — a thin, watery finish is the most common mistake.

Ingredients

Chicken

3 lbbone-in chicken thighs and drumsticks
1 Tbspolive oil
—salt and black pepper

Soffritto & Liquid

1 mediumonion, finely diced
4 clovesgarlic, sliced
½ cupdry white wine (Pinot Grigio or Vermentino)
14 ozwhole peeled tomatoes, crushed by hand
½ cupchicken stock

Aromatics

½ cupCastelvetrano or Kalamata olives, pitted and roughly torn
2 Tbspcapers, drained
2 sprigsfresh rosemary
3 sprigsfresh thyme
1 tspred chili flakes

Finish

handfulfresh flat-leaf parsley, roughly chopped
drizzleextra virgin olive oil

Method

1 — Sear

Pat chicken completely dry with paper towels — surface moisture prevents browning. Season generously on all sides with salt and black pepper.

Heat 1 Tbsp olive oil in a wide heavy pot over medium-high until just smoking. Place chicken skin-side down. Do not move it for 5 minutes — you want deep golden color, not pale beige. Flip and cook 2 minutes more. Remove to a plate.

Pour off all but approximately 1 Tbsp rendered fat from the pot.

2 — Soffritto & Deglaze

Reduce heat to medium. Add diced onion. Cook 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until soft and translucent. Add garlic, cook 1 minute until fragrant.

Add white wine. Scrape up every browned bit from the bottom of the pot — this is flavor. Let wine reduce by half, about 2 minutes.

3 — Braise

Add crushed tomatoes, stock, torn olives, capers, rosemary, thyme, and chili flakes. Stir to combine.

Return chicken skin-side up, nestling pieces into the sauce. Bring to a simmer. Reduce heat to low, cover partially (not fully — you need some evaporation). Cook 35 minutes.

4 — Reduce & Finish

Uncover. Increase heat to medium. Cook 15–20 minutes more, turning chicken once or twice, until the sauce is thick enough to coat the back of a spoon and the chicken is pulling visibly from the bone.

Taste before adding any salt — the olives and capers contribute significant sodium. Adjust if needed. Remove rosemary and thyme sprigs.

Scatter parsley over the top. Finish with a small drizzle of good olive oil. Serve directly from the pot.
