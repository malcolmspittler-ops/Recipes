Orange–Chipotle Chicken with Achiote

Boneless Skinless Thighs · Oven Braise → Shred → Broil · Taco-Ready
Yield ~6 servings
Braise 300°F · 55–65 min
Best Cut Boneless Skinless Thighs
Total ~90 min active

Ingredients

Chicken & Spice Rub

3 lbboneless skinless chicken thighs
2 tspkosher salt
1 tspground cumin
1 tspsmoked paprika
1 tspachiote paste
½ tspblack pepper
½ tspMexican oregano

Braising Liquid

1 orangeorange, zested
¾–1 cupfresh orange juice (2–3 oranges)
2chipotle peppers in adobo, minced
1 Tbspadobo sauce
4 clovesgarlic, smashed
½ cupchicken stock
1 Tbspneutral oil

Method

1 — Season & Sear

Preheat oven to 300°F.

Mix achiote paste into all the dry spices to form a rough paste. Pat chicken dry and rub evenly over all surfaces.

Heat Dutch oven over medium-high, add oil. Sear thighs 3–4 minutes on one side only until deep golden. Remove chicken. You're building real Maillard flavor here, not just light color — don't rush it.

2 — Build & Braise

Lower heat to medium. Add garlic, cook 30 seconds.

Add orange juice, zest, minced chipotle, adobo sauce, and stock. Scrape pot clean. Liquid should come no more than halfway up the meat — adjust stock volume if your vessel is wide.

Return chicken seared-side up in a single layer. Cover tightly.

Braise at 300°F:
Thighs: 55–65 minutes until fork-tender and easily pulled
Breasts: check at 30–35 minutes — pull before they tighten up

3 — Shred & Reduce

Transfer chicken to a sheet pan. Shred into large chunks — not fine. You want texture.

Bring braising liquid to a boil on the stovetop. Reduce uncovered until slightly syrupy and glossy, about 5–8 minutes.

Toss shredded chicken in the reduced sauce to coat.

4 — Broil Finish

Spread sauced chicken on sheet pan in a single layer.

Broil on high, 4–5 inches from element, 4–6 minutes until edges char lightly and surface caramelizes. Watch it — orange juice sugars burn fast.

Serve immediately.

Achiote note: If substituting ground annatto for achiote paste, use ½ tsp. Old or stale annatto can taste chalky — use fresh. If using paste (El Yucateco or similar), it already contains vinegar and garlic; reduce your smashed garlic to 2 cloves if you want to compensate.
