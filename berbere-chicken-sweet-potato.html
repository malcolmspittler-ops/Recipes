Berbere Chicken with Sweet Potato and Red Onion

Sheet Tray · Ethiopian-Inspired · One Pan
Serves4
MethodSheet Tray · 400°F
Total Time~70 min
Gluten-Free✓ Naturally

Ingredients

Chicken & Spice Paste

3 lbbone-in skin-on chicken thighs
3 Tbspberbere spice blend (store-bought)
2 Tbspolive oil
4 clovesgarlic, minced
½lemon, juiced
1 tspsalt

Vegetables

2 mediumsweet potatoes, cut into 1-inch cubes (about 4 cups)
2red onions, cut into thick wedges
1 Tbspolive oil (for sweet potato)
1 Tbspberbere (for sweet potato)

Finish

handfulfresh cilantro
—lemon wedges
optionalplain yogurt alongside
400°F not 425°F. Berbere contains fenugreek which turns bitter if it burns. Lower temperature than the harissa recipe gives the spice blend time to bloom and color without scorching. Don't substitute a higher temperature to speed things up.

Method — Sequencing

Sweet potato
Chicken
Red onion

1 — Sweet Potato First

Preheat oven to 400°F. Line a large sheet tray with foil.

Toss sweet potato cubes with 1 Tbsp olive oil, 1 Tbsp berbere, and a pinch of salt. Spread in a single layer on the tray. Roast 15 minutes alone — they need a head start or they won't caramelize before the chicken is done.

2 — Make Spice Paste & Coat Chicken

While sweet potato roasts, combine remaining 2 Tbsp berbere, 2 Tbsp olive oil, minced garlic, lemon juice, and salt. Mix into a thick paste. Coat chicken thighs thoroughly on all surfaces including under the skin where possible.

3 — Add Chicken and Onion

After 15 minutes, remove tray. Push sweet potato cubes to the edges. Add red onion wedges to the center of the tray. Nestle chicken thighs skin-side up among everything.

Return to oven. Roast 35–40 minutes more until chicken skin is crisp and deeply colored, sweet potato is caramelized at the edges and completely tender, and onion wedges have softened and charred at the tips.

4 — Rest and Serve

Rest 5 minutes — the sweet potato continues cooking from carryover heat. Scatter cilantro over everything. Serve directly from the tray with lemon wedges. Yogurt alongside cuts the heat of the berbere well.
