Pre-ground chicken breast goes chalky when cooked and lacks the fat needed for good texture. The small amount of thigh fat keeps the larb moist without meaningfully impacting sat fat.

Method

1 ‚Äî Toasted Rice Powder

Place raw jasmine rice in a dry skillet over medium heat. Stir constantly for 6‚Äì8 minutes until each grain turns deep golden and the kitchen smells nutty. Don't rush ‚Äî pale rice powder has no flavor.

Spread on a plate to cool completely. Grind to a coarse powder in a spice grinder or mortar ‚Äî aim for texture between sand and flour, not completely smooth. Set aside.

2 ‚Äî Dressing

Combine fish sauce, lime juice, sugar, and chili flakes in a small bowl. Stir until sugar dissolves. Taste it ‚Äî it should be aggressively salty, sour, and spicy at this stage. That's correct. It will balance once diluted by the chicken.

3 ‚Äî Cook Chicken

Heat a wide skillet over medium-high with no oil. Add ground chicken and break apart with a spoon or spatula.

Cook just until no pink remains ‚Äî do not brown it. You want pale, moist, loosely-textured meat, not caramelized crumbles. If significant liquid pools in the pan, drain it off but don't press the meat dry.

4 ‚Äî Dress & Finish

Remove pan from heat immediately. Pour dressing over the hot chicken. Add 2 Tbsp toasted rice powder. Toss thoroughly ‚Äî the residual heat is what makes the dressing absorb properly. Don't let it sit before tossing.

Fold in shallots, most of the mint, cilantro, and scallions. Reserve a small amount of each herb for garnish.

Taste and adjust. More lime juice if it tastes flat. More fish sauce if it needs salt. More chili if the heat isn't registering. The final dish should be bright, punchy, and herb-forward.

Serve at room temperature in lettuce or cabbage cups. Arrange cucumber slices alongside. Dust with remaining rice powder at the table. Squeeze fresh lime over each serving.

Timing note: Larb is best served within 20‚Äì30 minutes of dressing. The herbs wilt and the texture softens if it sits. If meal-prepping, store the cooked chicken and dressing separately, then combine and add herbs just before serving.
