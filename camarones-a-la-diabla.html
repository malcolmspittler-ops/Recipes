Method

1 ‚Äî Toast & Soak Chilis

Toast dried chilis in a dry skillet over medium heat, pressing flat with a spatula, 30 seconds per side until fragrant and slightly darkened. You want pliable and aromatic ‚Äî not black. Burned chilis make the sauce bitter and there is no fixing it.

Transfer toasted chilis to a bowl. Cover with boiling water and submerge with a plate or small lid. Soak 15 minutes until fully softened. Reserve ¬Ω cup soaking liquid ‚Äî don't discard it.

2 ‚Äî Build & Strain Sauce

Add softened chilis, reserved soaking water, tomatoes, garlic, chipotle, adobo sauce, cumin, and salt to a blender. Blend on high until completely smooth, at least 60 seconds.

Strain through a medium-mesh sieve into a bowl, pressing and scraping the solids through with a spoon. Discard what remains in the sieve ‚Äî mostly skins and seeds. The strained sauce should be smooth and deeply red.

3 ‚Äî Fry the Sauce

Heat 1 Tbsp oil in a wide skillet or saucepan over medium-high until shimmering. Pour in the strained sauce all at once ‚Äî it will splatter aggressively, have a splatter screen or loose lid ready.

Fry the sauce 5‚Äì7 minutes, stirring frequently, until it darkens noticeably and the smell shifts from raw chili to something toasty and concentrated. The sauce will thicken and reduce. This is the flavor-building step ‚Äî don't rush it or skip it.

4 ‚Äî Cook Shrimp

Reduce heat to medium. Season shrimp lightly with salt. Add to the sauce in a single layer.

Cook 2 minutes, flip each shrimp, cook 1 minute more. Pull off heat the moment they turn opaque ‚Äî residual heat will finish them. Overcooked shrimp in a spicy sauce cannot be fixed.

Squeeze lime juice over the top. Serve immediately with cilantro, raw white onion, and extra lime wedges alongside.

Serving suggestion: Excellent over white rice or cauliflower rice, which absorbs the sauce. Also works in warm corn tortillas as tacos. The raw white onion garnish is not optional ‚Äî the sharp bite cuts the richness of the sauce and is part of the dish.
